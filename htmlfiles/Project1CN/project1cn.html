Destination Overview

Paradise Island can refer to several idyllic destinations, but it most commonly denotes the resort-filled island in theBahamas connected to Nassau, known for luxury resorts, casinos, golf courses, and beaches like Cabbage Beach.

It can also refer to smaller, quieter islands, like the one in the Philippines near Port Barton, which features a more secluded, nature-focused experience with good snorkeling and staff-run hospitality. Across these locations, the name implies a beautiful, relaxing, and enjoyable getaway with a variety of activities.

Originally known as Hog Island, the area was renamed Paradise Island in the 1960s to reflect its picturesque charm and growing appeal as a tourist destination. The island is connected to Nassau by two bridges, making it easily accessible while still giving visitors the feeling of being far away from the busy city life. Its location in the Atlantic Ocean, surrounded by crystal-clear waters and coral reefs, makes it ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and other water adventures.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit
Year-round,but peak season is during the dry months from June to September.
consider visiting during weekdays for fewer crowds.

Currency
Local currency is Paradise Dollars (PD).
Major credit cards are widely accepted.

Language
English and Paradisean Creoleare widely spoken.
Learn a few local phrases to enhance your interaction with locals.

Local Cuisine
Indulge in the rich flavors of Paradise Island's local cuisine,featuring a fusion of seafood ,tropical fruits,and aromatic spices.
Try the traditional dish called "Island Delight"for true taste of local flavors

Weather Conditions
While the weather is generally sunny,occassionally shower can occur.It's adviceable to carry the raincoat or umbrella.
Check the weather forecast before planning outdoor activities
